I have been reading an extremely engrossing book - An Immense World by Ed Yong. 
It’s so power-packed with brilliant and mind blowing information that I haven’t been able to finish it off in a single go. 

Let me share central theme of this book. 

Every species on planet has its own major sensory ability. For us, humans, it’s vision. For dogs, as you know it’s smell. These senses allow us to experience our surroundings, our physical spaces and our environments. Million species, thousands of senses. From sight to texture, from sounds to vibrations, from smell to tastes, from electric fields to magnetic fields. 

But every specie can tap into a small fraction of reality’s fullness. Thereby making each one of us enclosed within own unique SENSORY BUBBLE. So basically each of us is perceiving a “tiny sliver of an immense world”.
